<Foundation Year w/ Integrated Science>
we did well in our alevels so were in the foundation year!!!
The Foundation Year program is one pathway–among many–to secure a degree in the Faculty of Science & Engineering. Candidates will spend one academic year to refine and develop skills in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry.

After the foundation year, successful candidates will have secured a place in the first year undergraduate program of their choice.

These programs include (but are not limited to) BS in Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence, Physics, Mathematics, and BE in Mechanical, Aerospace, Civil engineering.

A foundation year course is likely needed for you if you're from one of the following countries:

Bahrain Bangladesh Botswana Brazil China Egypt Indonesia Iran Japan Jordan Kenya Korea Kuwait Latin America (including Mexico) Nigeria Oman Pakistan Peru Qatar Russia Saudia Arabia Syria Taiwan Thailand Turkey United Arab Emirates Vietnam
<Foundation Year Super Units>
Super Unit 1

Maths OB1 or OC1 Maths OF2 Maths OB2 or OC2

The OB and OC series provides basic mathematics lessons. Units within the module consist of integral and differential calculus, sequences and series, and trigonometry.

The two series have different audiences: OB1 and 2 is for students with some post GCSE mathematics, OC1 and 2 none. That being said, the OB series cover more advanced materials compared to the other. Suffixes '1' and '2' indicate which semester the module will be taught.

The OF2 Mathematics course consist of two main modules, i.e. vectors and statistics.

Minimum 70%
Super Unit 2

Option 1 Option 2 Option 3

Computer Science students have the liberty of choosing these three optional subjects. Other schools may restrict these options to only Physics modules, or a combination of Physics and Chemistry modules.

Options 1 and 2 are to be taught in the first semester, whereas 3 in the second.

Minimum 60%
Super Unit 3

Maths OD2 Maths ON1 Maths OJ2

Mathematics OD2 covers numerical methods of calculation, this includes the Newton-Raphson method, linear and quadratic interpolation, etc. OF2, on the other hand, covers topics in mechanics, utilizing methods from different modules, mainly differential and integral calculus in addition to vectors.

Mathematics ON1 covers topics in pure mathematics, this includes mathematical induction, sets and boolean algebra.

Minimum 70%
Super Unit 4

ICT Academic Skills Project

This particular super unit is the same for all students. Computer Science candidates require an average of 60% in this super unit, and require at least 50% in each of these modules to achieve a 'pass' grade.

ICT and academic skills cover the same topics for all foundation year students. The project, however, is tailored to each different progression school.

For the school of computer science, students are required to create a website from scratch.

Minimum 60%
